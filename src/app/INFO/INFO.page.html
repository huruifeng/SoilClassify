2. About and Usage

 Introduction of Soil Classify App

The smart phone app is developed for soil classification. Geotechnical engineer (civil engineer) can use the app to classify the soil based on two soil classification systems AASHTO and USCS.

AASHTO System of Soil Classification
According to AASHTO classification, soil is classified into seven major groups: A-1 through A-7. Soils classified under groups A-1, A-2, and A-3 are granular materials of which 35% or less of the particles pass through the No. 200 sieve. Soils of which more than 35% pass through the No. 200 sieve are classified under groups A-4, A-5, A-6, and A-7. These soils are mostly silt and clay-type materials.
To classify a soil according to AASHTO classification system, one must input the following information:
  • Percentage of passing #10 sieve (2 mm opening), in percentage
  • Percentage of passing #40 sieve (0.425 mm opening), in percentage
  • Percentage of passing #200 sieve (0.0075 mm opening), in percentage
  • PercentageLiquid limit LL,
  • Plastic limit PL, or
  • Plasticity index PI (PI = LL - PL)
Then, click "Submit" to get the soil classification. The group index (GI) will be calculated by the app and shown inside parentheses.

Example: Determine the AASHTO soil classification for the inorganic soil with following information:
  • Percentage of passing #10 sieve (2 mm opening) = 92%
  • Percentage of passing #40 sieve (0.425 mm opening) = 74%
  • Percentage of passing #200 sieve (0.0075 mm opening) = 54% (=F = %P200)
  • Liquid limit LL = 44
  • Plastic limit PL = 21
Answer: A-7-6 (9)
Unified Soil Classification System (USCS)
It is a soil classification system used in engineering and geology to describe the texture and grain size of a soil. The classification system can be applied to most unconsolidated materials, and is represented by a two-letter symbol. First and/or second letters include G (Gravel), S (Sand), M (Silt), C (Clay), and O (Organic). The second letter include P (Poorly graded), W (Well-graded), H (High plasticity), and L (Low plasticity).
To classify a soil according to USCS classification system, one must input the following information:
  • Percentage of passing #4 sieve (4.75 mm opening), in percentage
  • Percentage of passing #200 sieve (0.0075 mm opening), in percentage
  • Liquid limit LL
  • Plastic limit PL, or
  • Plasticity index PI (PI = LL - PL)
  • Cu = coefficient of uniformity = D60/D10
  • Cc = coefficient of curvature = D302 / D60D10
  • D10 , in mm
  • D30 , in mm
  • D60 , in mm
Then, click "Submit" to get the soil classification.

Example 1: Determine the unified soil classification for the inorganic soil with following information:
  • Percentage of passing #4 sieve (4.75 mm opening) = 100% (= No. 4 sieve input)
  • Percentage of passing #200 sieve (0.0075 mm opening) = 4% (= No. 200 sieve input)
  • D10 = 0.1 mm
  • D30 = 0.17mm
  • D60 = 0.40 mm
Answer: SP - poorly graded sand

Example 2: Determine the unified soil classification for the inorganic soil with following information:
  • Percentage of passing #4 sieve (4.75 mm opening) = 97%
  • Percentage of passing #200 sieve (0.0075 mm opening) = 54%
  • Liquid limit LL = 44
  • Plastic limit PL = 21 (where PI = plasticity index = LL - PL)
Answer: CL - sandy lean clay

Example 3: Determine the unified soil classification for the inorganic soil with following information:
  • Percentage of passing #4 sieve (4.75 mm opening) = 80%
  • Percentage of passing #200 sieve (0.0075 mm opening) = 10%
  • Liquid limit LL = 30
  • Plastic limit PL = 25
  • D10 = 0.075 mm
  • D30 = 0.39mm
  • D60 = 1.7mm
Answer: SW-SM - well graded sand with silt & gravel
